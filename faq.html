FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

The Destiny 2 Mythic Challenge is the most hardcore way to play Destiny 2. In addition to the base game's rules, there are a few added restrictions to the way you are allowed to play. These restrictions make the game much more difficult, and are meant to test the gameplay capabilities of well versed and experienced players.

The goal of this challenge is to take a brand new Destiny 2 account all the way to Guardian Rank 11 - playing solo as much as possible. This means there is an artificial "seasonal" timer on how long you have to achieve Rank 11 before your progress is reset.

Permadeath
If the active Guardian is downed at any point, they must immediately return to character selection and delete that character, along with all gear on that character.

The nature of the death does not impact the result - whether it's PVE, PVP, or a Social Space, every death counts.

EXCEPTIONS
- Being calcified by The Witness during the Salvation's Edge Verity encounter is not considered a death.
- Loading into an activity in a "downed" state is not considered a death.

No HUD
The HUD is never allowed to be enabled at any point during play.

No Vault
Guardians are not allowed to use the vault in any manner. If any item is sent to the vault - be it intentional or automatic - it must be dismantled and cannot be added to a Guardian's inventory.

No Postmaster
Guardians are not allowed to use the postmaster in any manner. If any item is sent to the postmaster - be it intentional or automatic - it must be dismantled and cannot be added to a Guardian's inventory.

Master+ PVE Activities
When an activity presents a difficulty selection option to the player, only Master or higher may be selected as the difficulty.

If the player is unable to customize the difficulty to Master, they must use at least 1 Bane Modifier, and 2 Negative Modifiers on the highest difficulty they can.

Endgame activities (Raids and Dungeons) are the only activities that can be completed on any difficulty.

No Abandoning Activities
Once an activity has been started, players are not allowed to leave or abandon the activity until they achieve "Mission Complete."

If an activity is ever soft locked due to an issue with the game or Destiny 2 servers, players may reload the activity from the start to attempt it again. No other activity can be started at this time.

If an activity is ever soft locked due to a player's own misjudgment (E.g. cannot stun/finish a champion or under-leveled), they are not allowed to leave.

Limited Characters
At the start, only 1 Guardian may be created of a randomly selected class. After completing the following challenges, additional characters may be created:

- Complete the most recent Legendary Campaign.
- Complete any raid.

While you may have your own opinions on the Guardian Rank system as a whole, it does provide a nice checklist of activities that scale in difficulty to work my way through. And most importantly, it forces me to play all of the most difficult endgame activities with the added restrictions I put in place.

It's also a timed objective, since every season your Guardian Rank resets back to Rank 5. This means that not only do I have to complete the most difficult activities in the game, but I have to do them in a certain time period.

I like hard games, I like increasingly more difficult challenges, and I wanted to make this game the hardest version of itself I could.

Every accomplishment - every loot drop feels much more exciting to achieve this way when you could lose it all at a moment's notice.
